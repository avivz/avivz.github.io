Aviv Zohar

I am a Senior Lecturer (Assistant. Prof.) at The School of Engineering and Computer Science at The Hebrew University and The Chief Scientist at QED-it.

Research Interests

The Economics of Computational Systems and Protocols.
Cryptocurrencies, Multi-Agent Systems, Game Theory, Mechanism Design, Networks.

Highlights

The SPECTRE Protocol

Our protocol for a fast, scalable, and secure cryprocurrency. Joint work with Yonatan Sompolinsky and Yoad Lewenberg. See the Medium post, full paper, and an interview on Epicenter Bitcoin

Hijacking Bitcoin: Routing Attacks on Cryptocurrencies

Our paper on the the vulnerabilities of Bitcoin to attacks at the network routing level. Joint Work with Maria Apostolaki and Laurent Vanbever. See the project page at ETH, and the IEEE S&P '17 paper.

Optimal Selfish Mining

Our work on the (lack of) resilience of Bitcoin to strategic mining attacks. We find the optimal way miners can exploit the protocol for their own gain. Work with Ayelet Sapirstein and Yonatan Sompolinsky. Paper from FC'16.
